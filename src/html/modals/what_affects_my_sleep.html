We’ve looked at whether the data you’ve recorded seems correlated with other factors. If the magnitude of the correlation is over 0.8 we say that there’s a strong relationship. If it’s over 0.5 we say there’s a weak relationship. Otherwise we say there’s no relationship.

Correlation is a simple measure of the relationship between two variables. It can easily fail to detect a more complicated relationship. Also a high correlation between two factors does not necessarily indicate that one factor is causing the other. They might, for example, be effects with a common cause.
